Battery Hog vs Activity Monitor

Activity Monitor is already excellent—and already on every Mac. Battery Hog is useful when your question is specifically about battery drain and the workload story behind it.

Short answer

Use Activity Monitor for a deep, general-purpose view of processes, CPU, memory, energy impact, disk, and network activity. Use Battery Hog when you want total battery-side watts, runtime context, project-level development workloads, thermal history, charge history, and sleep-blocker guidance in one focused investigation.

What both tools cannot honestly promise

Neither should be described as a perfect per-app watt meter. Battery Hog’s live watts represent the whole Mac; its app and workload ranking uses other signals. A process that is active at the same moment as high draw may be a contributor without being the only cause.

Why use both?

Battery Hog can tell you that a project workload is a likely contributor. Activity Monitor can then help you inspect an individual process in depth. They are complements more often than replacements.

Choose Activity Monitor alone if: you only need an occasional process lookup, want zero additional software, or need broad system inspection more than a focused battery narrative.
