Battery Hog vs WhatBattery

WhatBattery is oriented around battery health, live battery information, chargers, connected devices, and history. Battery Hog is oriented around diagnosing the apps and development workloads behind an unplugged drain.

The deciding question

If you are asking “How is my battery and charger doing?”, explore WhatBattery. If you are asking “What is my Mac doing right now, and which project workload is involved?”, Battery Hog is designed for that investigation.

Choose the narrower story you need

Battery tools overlap because battery level, health, cycles, and draw are useful foundations. The real distinction is what they help you do next. Battery Hog’s differentiator is the path from whole-Mac draw to project and process context—not a claim to replace every battery-health utility.
